Diesel Electric Locomotives

Class M2

Class M2 is a diesel locomotive made by General Motors Diesel, Canada, imported in 1954 under the "Colombo Plan". It's considered one of the most successful locomotives in Sri Lanka.

Class M4

Class M4 locomotives were supplied by Montreal Locomotive Works, Canada, and imported in the 1970s. They are primarily used for freight services and are known for their robustness.

Class M5

Class M5 locomotives were manufactured by Hitachi, Japan, and arrived in the late 1970s. Mainly used for passenger trains, they are appreciated for their high power output and performance on steep gradients.

Class M6

Class M6 locomotives were built by Thyssen-Henschel in West Germany in the late 1970s. They handle both passenger and freight duties and are renowned for their durability.

Class M7

Class M7 locomotives were introduced in the late 1980s and were built by brush Traction, UK. It is the shortest and least powerful locomotive in SLR.

Class M8

Class M8 locomotives were imported from India in the 1990s. They are used mainly for freight and mixed services and are recognized for their strength and dependability.

Class M9

Class M9 locomotives were imported in the year 2000 from France. Only a few are operational due to various problems.

Class M10

Class M10 locomotives are from India and entered service in the early 2010s. They are used for heavy freight and express passenger trains and have proven to be reliable.

Class M11

Class M11 locomotives were imported from India in the late 2010s, This is the strongest locomotive in the SLR fleet.

Other Locomotives

Class W2

Class W2 locomotives were built by LEW Henningsdorf in Germany in the late 1960s.

Class W3

Class W1 locomotives were built by Thyssen-Henschell in West Germany in the late 1960s. They were later modified into the current Class W3.

Class Y

Locomotive built by the Hunslet Locomotive Company, Only used of Shunting.

Railbus

Built from TATA and Ashok Leyland Busses with minimal modifications, in the 1990s to 2000s. Still operational today.
