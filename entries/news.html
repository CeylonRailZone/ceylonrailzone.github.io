Most of the Sri Lanka's Railway infrastructure was damaged and is in inoperable condition after the catertrophic Cyclone Ditwah. It has caused many landslides, floods and heavy winds resulting in Railway Washaways, Bridge collapses and debris on the tracks. While these may seem simple to get back and running, It is a difficuilt task especially in the Upcountry line due to the rough terrain of the area.

Visit the official Sri Lanka Railways website for up-to-date information

Coastal Line, Kalani Valley Line

Coastal line, from Colombo to Beliatta, and Kalani Valley line, from Colombo to Awissawella, was not affected by the tragedy, So it is still in operational condition.

Main Line

Main line from Colombo to Ambepussa was not affected.

Ambepussa to Badulla section was initially not operational, But due to the immense work of SLR workers, Colombo to Rambukkana and Ambewela to Badulla sections are back in operation.

Rambukkana to Ambewela section, as well as the Matale line is still not operational.

Northern Line

Northern line was not operational just after the tragedy, but now it is in operational contidion.

Puttalam Line

Puttalam Line from Colombo to Nattandia was not affected. Nattandia to Puttalam section is now under construction.

Eastern Lines

Eastern Lines from Maho to Trincomalee and Batticaloa were affected, but now in operational condition.

Mannar Line

Mannar line from Madawachchiya to Talaimannar was affected and is still not in operation.
